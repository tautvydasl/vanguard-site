The History Behind It

After signing the Molotov–Ribbentrop Pact, Nazi Germany and the Soviet Union carve up Eastern Europe into spheres of influence. Lithuania falls under Soviet control and is occupied. Mass deportations to Siberia begin. Young men start gathering in the forests in small groups, and more and more people join the fight for freedom and independence. Partisan units form across different regions of the country.

A growing network of supporters helps them hide, gathers intelligence, and supplies the equipment and gear they need to keep fighting the occupiers. The partisan movement becomes a serious problem for the occupying forces, who spread propaganda to discredit the partisans — the partisans answer in kind, spreading their own propaganda calling people to fight for Lithuania and freedom.

With so few resources, the Forest Brothers often have no choice but to raid Soviet troops — seizing weapons, vehicles, and other supplies, or hitting key targets outright. Soviet forces vastly outnumber them, so unity and cunning are everything. For a free Lithuania.
